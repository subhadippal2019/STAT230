\DefBoxOne{ \begin{center} \bf Related to World Reserve Life Index (WRLI)\end{center}\vspace{-.25in}}{\color{black}
'World Reserve Life Index' provides an estimate for the years remaining before a specific world resource is expected to be completely depleted based on certain assumptions.  Below are the related formula:
}\\

\ThmBox{
\color{black}{\bf World Reserve Life Index (WRLI):}
If the the consumption remains fixed as it was in the year  $T$, then the Number years of the year  for a complete depletion of the Resource is:\\
\TextInBoxTwo[white!25]{5.5in}{$$\text{WRLI}_{\HLTY{\text{T}}}= \frac{\text{ World Reserve at the End of a Year \HLTY{\text{T}}}}{ \text{World Consumption in the Year \HLTY{\text{T}}}}$$}\vspace{.05in}\\
$$\text{WR{\bf B}LI}_{\HLTY{\text{T}}}= \frac{\text{ World {\bf Base} Reserve at the End of a Year \HLTY{\text{T}}}}{ \text{World Consumption in the Year \HLTY{\text{T}}}}$$
}{}\\

\vspace{.1in}
\ThmBox{\color{black}{\bf World Reserve Life Index (WRLI):}
If the the consumption {\bf Increases } by a Annual Average Growth Rate of  \HLTY{\text{AAGR}}\% from the year $T$,  then the Number years of the year for a complete depletion of the Resource is: \\
\TextInBoxTwo[white!25]{5.5in}{
$$ \text{WRLI}^{\text{*}}_{_{\HLTY{\text{T, {\tiny AAGR}}}}}  = \frac{\log\left(1+\text{WRLI}_{\HLTY{\text{T}}}\times \frac{\text{AAGR}}{100+\text{AAGR}} \right) }{\log\left( \frac{100+\text{AAGR}}{\text{100}} \right)  } $$}\\
}\\

\vspace{.1in}

\ThmBox{\color{black}{\bf World Reserve Life Index (WRLI):}
If the the consumption {\bf Decreases } by a Annual Average  Rate of  \HLTY{\text{AAGR}}\% from the year $T$,  then the Number years of the year for a complete depletion of the Resource is:
\begin{itemize}
\item\TextInBoxTwo[white!25]{5.5in}{ If  $\left(1-\text{WRLI}_{\HLTY{\text{T}}}\times \frac{\text{AAGR}}{100-\text{AAGR}} \right)>0$, then  \\
$$ \text{WRLI}^{\text{*}}_{_{\HLTY{\text{T, {\tiny AAGR}}}}}  = \frac{\log\left(1-\text{WRLI}_{\HLTY{\text{T}}}\times \frac{\text{AAGR}}{100-\text{AAGR}} \right) }{\log\left( \frac{100-\text{AAGR}}{\text{100}} \right)  } $$}
\item  $\left(1-\text{WRLI}_{\HLTY{\text{T}}}\times \frac{\text{AAGR}}{100-\text{AAGR}} \right)\leq 0$, then the World Reserve on the material is never going to be depleted if we continue to decrease consumption at the specified rate. Eventually (hypothetically) the consumption will be almost zero before the resource gets depleted.   \\

*{\tiny The above calculation is based on the assumption that $AAGR\%<100\%$. }
\end{itemize}
}\\
