\DefBoxOne{ \begin{center} \bf Related to Regression\end{center}\vspace{-.25in}}{\color{black}
Let $(X_1, Y_1), (X_2, Y_2), \ldots , (X_n, Y_n)$ are sijmple random sample of size n of the variabes $X,Y$. $X$ is the covariate and $Y$ is the response variable. \\
\TextInBoxTwo[white!25]{5.5in}{
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ |c|| c|c|}
\hline
Attribute & Notation & Formula\\
\hline
Sample Mean  of X &$ \overline{X}$ &  $\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n X_i$ \\
\hline
Sample Mean  of Y &$ \overline{Y}$ &  $\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n Y_i$ \\
\hline
Sample Standard Deviation  of X &$ S_{_X}$ &   $\frac{1}{n-1}\sum_{i=1}^n (X_i-\overline{X} )^2$ \\
\hline
Sample Standard Deviation  of Y &$ S_{_Y}$ &   $\frac{1}{n-1}\sum_{i=1}^n (Y_i-\overline{Y} )^2$ \\
\hline
Covariance between X and Y &$ S_{_{XY}}$ &   $\frac{1}{n-1}\sum_{i=1}^n (X_i-\overline{X} )(Y_i-\overline{Y} )$ \\
\hline
Correlation between X and Y &$ r_{_{XY}}$ &   $\frac{ S_{_{XY}}}{ S_{_X} S_{_Y}}$ \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
}\\

\vspace{.2in}
Let us consider a linear regression line $\HLTEQ[white]{\hat{Y}=a+bX}$, where $Y $ is the response variable, $X$ is the independent variable or Covariate.  $\HLTEQ[white]{a}$ is called the {\bf  `intercept'} and $\HLTEQ[white]{b}$ is called the {\bf `slope'} of the fitted line.  The formula for the estimated value of a and b is given as below: 
$$\HLTEQ[white]{ \hat{b}= r_{_{X,Y}}\frac{S_{_Y}}{S_{_X}}}\hspace{.2in}\text{ and }\HLTEQ[white]{\hat{a}= \overline{Y}-  \hat{b}\overline{X}}$$\\
Note that $S_{_Y}$ and $S_{_X}$ are always a positive number. Where as,  the correlation coefficient between $X$ and $Y$, $r_{_{XY}}$  is a number between -1 to 1.
}\\


\DefBoxOne{ \begin{center} \bf Gini's Formula\end{center}\vspace{-.25in}}{\color{black}
There are several ways to compute Gini coefficient however it is often calculated with Brown Formula given by:
 $$G = 100\% - \left(\frac{\sum [f_i \times (y_i+y_{i+1})]}{10,000} \times 100\right)\% $$
 where $f_i$ = percentage decile shares of world population. $y_i=$  cumulative percentage shares of world product for percentile groupings. An Example of Gini's Table from Slide

\DefBoxOne{ \begin{center} \bf Confidence Interval \end{center}\vspace{-.25in}}{\color{black}
\TextInBoxTwo[white!25]{6.5in}{
Let $X_1, X_2, \ldots , X_n$ be observed random {\bf sample of size $n$}.  Let $\overline{X}$ denotes the sample Mean while $S_{_{X}}$ refers to the sample Standard deviation. Then, assuming the population has a fine variance and the sample size $n$ to be a large number,  a formula for confidence interval for the population mean is given as:
$$\left[ \overline{X}- z_{_{_\text{Cutoff}}}\times \frac{S_{_X}}{\sqrt{n}},  \hspace{.5in}  \overline{X}+ z_{_{_\text{Cutoff}}}\times \frac{S_{_X}}{\sqrt{n}} \right]$$
$z_{_{_\text{Cutoff}}}$ is determined based on the confidence-level of the interval.  The values of $z_{_{_\text{Cutoff}}} $ can be obtained from the following table: 
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|l|}
\hline
  Confidence Level& 90\% & 95\% & 99\% \\ \hline \hline
$z_{_{_\text{Cutoff}}}$ &  1.645  & 1.96     &2.576    \\ \hline\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
}
